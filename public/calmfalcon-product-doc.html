Vision

Become the intelligent layer between enterprise AI teams and their product workflows — eliminating manual AI ecosystem research and enabling faster, evidence-backed decisions.

Goal

Using multi-agent AI, CalmFalcon automatically ingests signals from GitHub releases, Exa web search (vendor docs, news, newsletters, research), and trend analysis queries, clusters them into high-confidence themes, and delivers a weekly brief that enterprise AI Platform Leaders can act on.

We aim to be the most trusted AI signal intelligence platform for enterprise platform and engineering teams.

User Journey — How It Works for You

Every Monday morning, CalmFalcon delivers a ready-to-read intelligence brief. Here's what your experience looks like:

Step 1
Log In

Sign in with your email or Google account. You land on your personalized dashboard.

Step 2
See This Week's Themes

Your dashboard shows 6–8 theme cards — each one is a key AI development from the past week. Every card shows a short title, whether you need to act now or keep watching, a confidence score (how strong the evidence is), and how many sources back it up.

Step 3
Filter What Matters to You

Want to focus on only the most urgent items? Filter by ACT (needs action now) or WATCH (worth tracking). You can also sort by confidence level to see the strongest signals first.

Step 4
Dig Into a Theme

Click any card to see the full picture: a 4–5 sentence summary of what happened, "Why It Matters" for your team, "What You Can Do" with 2–3 concrete next steps, and every source cited with its URL and publication date so you can verify it yourself. If the theme appeared last week too, you'll see how it's trending.

Step 5
Download the Weekly Brief

Grab a ready-to-share brief (≤700 words) that you can forward directly to your CTO or engineering org. It includes an executive summary, the top themes ranked by confidence, and a watch list of signals to track over the next 30 days. No rewriting needed.

Step 6
Compare Week Over Week

View past weekly briefs side by side. See which themes are new, which are continuing, and how confidence scores are changing — so you can tell if a trend is accelerating or fading before your next planning cycle.

How We Evaluate

Every pipeline run is evaluated by 4 methods. Each catches different problems.

Manual HHH Review

22 yes/no questions per theme

Measures: quality, trust, safety

LLM Judge

GPT-4 Turbo scores 1–5 on 4 dimensions

Measures: relevance, accuracy, writing

Automated Checks

13 deterministic rules (no LLM)

Measures: structure, formatting, constraints

Pipeline Health

Operational & signal quality metrics

Measures: speed, coverage, accuracy

Set Up n8n Cloud

Sign up at n8n.io and create a new workspace. No installation needed.

4

Add Workflow Variables

Settings → Variables: OPENAI_API_KEY, EXA_API_KEY, GITHUB_TOKEN, SUPABASE_URL, SUPABASE_KEY

5

Import Main Pipeline

… → Import from File → calmfalcon-signals-pipeline-multi-agent.json. Then Cmd+A → Tidy Up.

Import Eval Pipelines

Import all 3 eval workflows separately:

  • calmfalcon-eval-automated.json — 13 automated checks (production)

Connect OpenAI Credentials

Click each LLM node (Theme Selector, Theme Writer, Verifier, Brief Writer, Judge) → select your OpenAI credential.

8

Test Run

Key Learnings

10 takeaways from building a multi-agent AI pipeline across 40 runs.

  1. One big AI agent failed, four small ones worked. Splitting one do-everything agent into four specialists (select, write, verify, summarize) eliminated made-up product names and citation hallucinations.
  2. Bad data in = bad results out, no matter how good your prompts are. Switching our web search provider fixed more issues than weeks of prompt tweaking because the old one was feeding us wrong dates.
  3. Don't trust AI to merge duplicates. Use code instead. Asking the LLM to "don't repeat vendors" worked sometimes, failed other times. A simple code rule would've been 100% reliable.
  4. Keep it simple. Users didn't need five categories. I pivoted to two: act now or watch later. Simplifying our signal classification from five types to just ACT/WATCH made the product instantly clearer and less complex.
  5. Don't rely only on the LLM's own judgment about how confident it is. Instead, add a rule-based or formula-based scoring layer in your application code to judge output quality more reliably.
  6. Filtering out noise is harder than writing good content. The AI writes great summaries for irrelevant topics. The real challenge is keeping junk out before it reaches the writer.
  7. Run tasks in parallel, check quality in sequence. Writing 8 themes simultaneously cut our pipeline from 8 minutes to 3. Quality checks still need to see all themes together.
  8. A scoring checklist beats "does this feel right?" Our 22-question rubric turned vague feedback into specific, fixable problems with clear before/after tracking.
  9. Accuracy is fixable with rules. Usefulness depends on your inputs. We got factual accuracy from 53% to 98% through mechanical fixes, but usefulness depends on whether the right signals were ingested in the first place.
  10. Prompt engineering gets you halfway. The rest is an engineering problem. Going from 0% to 50% quality was all prompt work. Getting from 50% to 80% requires fixing the data pipeline in code.
